Heritage

Historical Landmarks

Walk through centuries of history — from colonial fortresses to ancient temples.

Heritage

Batticaloa Dutch Fort

Built 1628 by the Portuguese, expanded by the Dutch. Four bastions, VOC gate emblem, panoramic lagoon views.

Worship

Hindu Temples & Mosques

Ancient kovils with intricate Dravidian architecture and historic mosques reflecting Batticaloa's diverse heritage.

Colonial

Colonial Churches

Dutch and British-era churches with unique architecture standing as testament to colonial history.

Beaches & Lagoon

Coastal Paradise

From calm lagoon waters to ocean beaches — Batticaloa offers it all.

Featured Beach

Pasikudah Bay

Crystal-clear turquoise waters stretching for kilometers. One of Sri Lanka's safest and most beautiful beaches. Perfect for swimming, snorkeling, jet skiing, and sailing. 35km from Batticaloa town with luxury resorts and beachside restaurants.

Kallady Beach

City beach, seafood, bridge sunsets

Vakarai Beach

Untouched, 50km north, solitude

Nature & Wildlife

Wild Batticaloa

Unique ecosystems — from boat safaris to mangrove forests.

Safari

Gal Oya National Park

Sri Lanka's only boat safari park — elephants swimming between islands, crocodiles, 150+ bird species.

Ecology

Mangrove Forests

Kayak through pristine mangrove tunnels — home to diverse birdlife and marine ecosystems.

Birding

Lagoon Birdwatching

100+ species including kingfishers, herons, cormorants, and migratory birds.
